---
title: Documentation Design Principles
---

Foundational guidelines for creating effective, user-centered documentation that serves both technical writers and developers.

<CardGrid columns={3}>

<Card title="User-Centered" titleColor="blue" backgroundColor="blue">
  Documentation should prioritize the user's goals and mental model
  over the system's internal structure, meeting them in the context
  and surface where they work.
</Card>

<Card title="Scannable" titleColor="green" backgroundColor="green">
  Documentation should use clear headings, bullet points, and visual hierarchy
  to help users quickly find what they need.
</Card>

<Card title="Actionable" titleColor="purple" backgroundColor="purple">
  Documentation should enable users to accomplish a specific task or understand
  a concept with clear, practical guidance.
</Card>

<Card title="Quality-Driven" titleColor="red" backgroundColor="red">
  Documentation should meet a measurable bar of quality across accuracy,
  relevance, completeness, conciseness, consistency, and discoverability.
</Card>

<Card title="Contributor-Friendly" titleColor="yellow" backgroundColor="yellow">
  Documentation should be easy for anyone to contribute to, with low barriers
  that keep content accurate and current.
</Card>

<Card title="AI-Ready" titleColor="gray" backgroundColor="gray">
  Documentation should be structured for AI consumption and leverage AI signals
  like citations, corrections, and sentiment to maintain content over time.
</Card>

</CardGrid>
